Prometheus

Subsystems

Intake

Prometheus dsipalyed an active intake. Using surgical tubing rollers and pocketted plates, includign a carbon fibere base plate. The intake used all plastic chains, as they are weight efficient while effective, and are best suited for the context of this subsystem. The active intake is attatched to misumi slides, allowing for horizontal extension and movement. The intake itself is doubel jointed, int eh way that one half is attatched to the slides ina fixed position, whilst the otehr, the one which actually intakes, can be moved slightly up and down and positioned, via servos

Powered by two motors with a 5:1 gear ratio, this subsystem was refined over three prototypes to reduce weight while increasing pixel retention.

Outtake

The outtake was built for precision placement with a linear slide and pivoting claw for vertical alignment with the drone board.

This design won regional praise for being lightweight and responsive, thanks to its compact 3D-printed mounting and servo array.

Hang

Hang uses a dual-motor lift with steel cabling and lock latches for high-weight capacity and quick field reset.

Originally too slow, it was upgraded with higher torque motors and reverse braking mechanisms to withstand full match duration strain.

Drive Base

Our drive base was designed for speed and traction, using 4" stealth wheels with a 13:3 gear ratio. To prevent tipping while climbing the drone board, the base features back and front stoppers, and all cabling is looped for easy maintenance.

Following regionals, we cut the base to allow for easier intake feeding, and added a weight bar for better center of mass. The result was a faster, more reliable drive train with improved pathing and fewer stuck states.
